\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}

We studied whether a task-grounded uncertainty framework can improve LLM-based
reranking under a controlled same-candidate protocol. \method{} turns LLM
evidence into a calibrated candidate relevance posterior, estimates
uncertainty, and applies a validation-controlled ranking family whose risk term
is audited rather than assumed necessary. Across Sports, Toys, Home, and Tools,
it ranks first against eight official-code-level baselines on HR/NDCG/MRR, with
all paired Holm-corrected tests positive and significant in each domain.

The diagnostic modules refine this result. Event-level uncertainty stratifies
ranking reliability, supporting the motivation for actionable uncertainty.
Component ablations and hyperparameter analyses show a more nuanced picture:
some uncertainty terms are weak or redundant, and positive risk penalty is not
necessary in the current sweeps. This honesty is part of the contribution. The
paper supports a scoped claim that C-CRP improves controlled same-candidate
ranking while uncertainty stratifies reliability, not a universal full-catalog
recommender SOTA result.
